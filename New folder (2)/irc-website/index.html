The Indian Rover Challenge was started in 2018, it is the first ever robotics and space exploration based competition to ignite and encourage the spirit of innovation amongst young engineers set on a quest to know about our ever expanding, unimaginable and mystic universe. The competition facilitates a chance to deepen their knowledge in one of the most interesting fields as they broaden their horizons and get hands-on experience on space technology by observing the struggle of robots on a simulated planet. The ideas proposed here are not just limited to this challenge, they can be extended in various ways because the birth of the space age has spurred on a plethora of new ideas and ground-breaking technologies that are used in day-to-day living.

IRC 2019 will be held from 9th January 2019 to 12th January 2019. The competition is presented by Manipal Academy of Higher Education and Mahindra & Mahindra. It will be held at Manipal Institute of Technology, Manipal.

This Would Not be possible without the support of our sponsors. The Sponosors support have allowed us to bring to you tthe second Edition of the Indian Rover Challenge, part of the rover challenge series.

IRC, Indian Rover Challenge is now a part of the rover challenge series, powered by Mars Society. First edition of IRC was hosted by Vellore Institute of Technology, Tamil Nadu in January 2018. We are pleased to inform you that the second edition of IRC will be hosted by Manipal Academy of Higher Education. Rover Challenge Series includes University Rover Challenge(URC), European Rover Challenge Series(ERC), UK University Rover Challenge(UKURC) and Canadian International Rover Challenge(CIRC). The aim of Rover Challenge Series is to challenge teams to design and build a rover that would be use of early explorers on mars. The students are encouraged to develop skills in robotics, improve state of art in rovers and work in multi-disciplinary teams.
